HB1001 - State Budget

Passed House

Bill Summary

House Bill 1001 is the state budget bill that makes significant changes to education funding in Indiana.

Key Provisions

  • Removes income maximums for choice scholarship eligibility
  • Changes curricular materials funding
  • Provides state funding for adult charter schools
  • Requires reporting on dual credit alignment
  • Prohibits excessive fees for career scholarship students
  • Mandates more frequent distribution of choice scholarships
  • Requires the Commission for Higher Education to report on facility utilization

Current Status

The bill has passed the House and is now being considered in the Senate.

Call Script

When calling your representative about this bill, consider using the following script:

“Hello, my name is [Your Name] and I’m a constituent at [Your Street Address, City, State, Zip Code].

I’m calling to express my opposition to House Bill 1001, the state budget bill. This bill would reduce public education funding through choice scholarships and significantly cut curricular materials funds, which I find deeply concerning.

Strong public education is essential for our state’s future, and I urge [Rep./Sen. Name] to support increased investment in our schools and educators rather than these cuts.

Thank you for your time.”
