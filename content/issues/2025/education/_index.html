I would appreciate hearing [Rep./Sen. Name]'s position on these bills. Thank you for your time." --- The House Republicans have made it a priority to trim Indiana's education code. In doing so, they are [removing the requirements](https://indianapublicmedia.org/news/deregulation-bill-would-trim-licensure-education-requirements-for-indiana-secretary-of-education.php) to be the secretary of education. The bill also removes requirements that a governing body of a school corporation that [operates a charter school must also operate a public school](https://www.wboi.org/2025-01-22/lawmakers-debate-changes-to-charter-school-rules-secretary-of-education-in-deregulation-bill). Education funding will see an elimination of the Curricular Material Reimbursement Fund, which was used to provide curricular reimbursements to public school and certain nonpublic school students. In Fiscal Year 2025 this amounted to $160 million. The wealthiest Hoosiers are able to save money while public schools could see more cuts to their budgets as the annual income maximum for choice scholarships is removed.
