Education

The House Republicans have made it a priority to trim Indianaâ€™s education code. In doing so, they are removing the requirements to be the secretary of education. The bill also removes requirements that a governing body of a school corporation that operates a charter school must also operate a public school.

Education funding will see an elimination of the Curricular Material Reimbursement Fund, which was used to provide curricular reimbursements to public school and certain nonpublic school students. In Fiscal Year 2025 this amounted to $160 million. The wealthiest Hoosiers are able to save money while public schools could see more cuts to their budgets as the annual income maximum for choice scholarships is removed.

Related Bills

HB 1001 - State Budget
Removes the income maximums for choice scholarship eligibility, change curricular materials funding, provide state funding for adult charter schools, require reporting on dual credit alignment, prohibit excessive fees for career scholarship students, mandate more frequent distribution of choice scholarships, and require the Commission for Higher Education to report on facility utilization at state educational institutions.
Passed House
HB 1002 - Various education matters
The document outlines a legislative bill that removes and repeals numerous education provisions across 62 different areas while amending or relocating certain provisions related to charter schools, governance, administration, and educational programs.
Passed House

Call Script

When calling your representative about education issues, consider using the following script:

"Hello, my name is [Your Name] and I'm a constituent at [Your Street Address, City, State, Zip Code].

I'm calling to express my opposition to House Bills 1001. This bill would reduce public education funding through choice scholarships and significantly cut curricular materials funds, which I find deeply concerning.

I also want to voice my concerns about House Bill 1002. I believe the Secretary of Education position requires substantial education experience to effectively lead Indiana's schools.

Strong public education is essential for our state's future, and I urge [Rep./Sen. Name] to support increased investment in our schools and educators rather than these cuts.

[Additional responses as needed:]

  • I would appreciate hearing [Rep./Sen. Name]'s position on these bills.

Thank you for your time."

Tip: Be respectful. The person you talk to is unlikely to be the Representative or Senator themselves.

Healthcare (example)

Healthcare remains a priority issue for Indiana legislators. The state has been working to improve access to healthcare for all residents, including expanding healthcare service availability in rural areas and increasing funding for mental health services.

Recent focus has been placed on telehealth services, addressing the opioid crisis, healthcare workforce development, and innovative models to lower costs while maintaining quality care.

Related Bills

SB 5 - Rural Healthcare Access
Provides incentives for healthcare providers to establish practices in underserved rural areas.
Passed
HB 1127 - Mental Health Services Expansion
Increases funding for community mental health centers and expands services for at-risk populations.
In Progress

Call Script

When calling your representative about healthcare issues, consider using the following script:

"Hello, my name is [Your Name] and I'm a constituent from [Your City/Town].

I'm calling to express my support for expanding healthcare access across Indiana, especially in rural areas. I urge support for bills like SB 5 and HB 1127.

Access to quality healthcare is essential for all Hoosiers, and I believe we need to ensure everyone can receive the care they need regardless of where they live.

Will [Representative Name] commit to supporting these healthcare initiatives?

Thank you for your time."

Tip: If you have personal experience with healthcare access challenges, briefly sharing your story can make your call more impactful.
